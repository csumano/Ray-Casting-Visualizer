2D to 3D Drawing Canvas
Purpose:
This project demonstrates an important computer graphics concept, Ray Casting. Ray casting solves various 3D problems with the use of intersection tests. In this project you are the eye and the canvas is a 3D space and the ray cast is the mouse. Clicking will determine a (x,y,z) cooridinate in 3D space to create a visible point. A segment is drawn from the first ray cast to the next in (x,y) space. Pressing shift will stop the ray cast. We can take these (x,y,z) cooridinates to calculate a three dimensional space to create a solid geometric object. The user can also turn on and off the ray case segments to visualize how it works.
Directions:
CLICK MOUSE - to create a point(s)
SHIFT - button to stop drawing segments
Press the 'Make3D' button to turn the segments into 3D
- Afterwards use the mouse to click on the canvas and move around the world space
Press the 'AddSegments' to draw more lines (world movement is fixed)
Press the 'RayCast' to see visible ray cast lines (indicated with an arrow)
Press the 'RemoveRayCast' to remove and stop ray cast lines
Press the 'reset' button to reset the canvas
